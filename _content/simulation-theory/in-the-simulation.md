# In the Simulation

Do we already live in a simulation? I'm sure almost everybody has asked
themselves that question before, right? We get into situations that feel like
glitches in the fabric of reality and start to wonder. But are we?

There is one theory in particular that holds up surprisingly well.

## The Theory

If we can create a simulation ourselves

- that is autonomous (not steered by somebody)
- that contains thinking entities
- those entities have the capacity themselves to create a simulation

then we are dealing with a chain, where we have knowledge of only two of the
links. And in a chain the likelihood that we are the link at the beginning is
much smaller than being in the middle. If simulations vastly outnumber base
realities, then statistically, any given conscious entity is almost certainly
inside a simulation.

Therefore the conclusion has to be that the likelihood that we are also living
in a simulation is significantly high.

([Nick Bostrom's _Simulation Argument_ from 2003](https://simulation-argument.com/simulation/))

## How Close Are We?

Now we are on the edge of producing AGI, artificial general intelligence. We
already have produced intelligence that is almost indistinguishable from human
intelligence in some situations. The next step would be to bring such AI actors
into an appropriate environment and we have created the next link in the chain.

So how far are we away? Ten years? And some research team will definitely do it.
Or probably Hollywood will produce a special kind of reality TV, where we watch
AI bots interact with each other.

But independent of the highly complex ethical implications, these "creatures"
will surely be as intelligent as we are and could of course come up with the
next link in the chain themselves. Then the theory already proves itself. The
chain is in motion.

The question is: Is the possibility that next links in the chain might exist in
the future not already enough to prove the theory? Can or should we already
assume that we live in a simulation?

## Consequences and Ideas

Then the logical next conclusion is that we definitely have a creator and that
nothing is what it seems. If our creators can fabricate physical evidence, the
age of our universe becomes an open question and so does physics in general. We
could actually have been created 5000 years ago as the creationists say and all
the proof that our world is older could be fabricated by those creators.

Now let's dive into stories and tropes and see what can be made out of it:

- **Doors:** It's obvious, right? We are curious. If there's another world, be
  it upward or downward, we want to explore it.
- **Observation:** I mentioned it already. we would never create another world
  and leave it be. We want to watch. We want to see their life patterns, their
  decisions, their evolution. But then we also should be aware that somebody is
  also watching us all the time. Not necessarily me or you specifically. That
  might be a relatively boring Truman Show. But the contemporary historic events
  definitely. Documented by a scientist or consumed by a popcorn-eating reality
  TV fan.
- **Workforce:** We create AI to do work for us as we speak. It's not what we
  expected. They don't clean up or do the taxes for us just yet. But AI agents
  help us. So a simulation most likely also has a purpose beyond curiosity. It
  could be there to find out more about psychological dynamics. Or we could
  bring the most brilliant AI models together to discuss Quantum Theory or cure
  cancer forever.
- **Ethical questions:** As said before, it's complicated. But questions arise
  immediately: Are we dealing with real life inside that simulation? Could we
  just shut it off? Should it be forbidden to manipulate the world in which the
  beings one chain down are living? I'm thinking of adding software routines in
  the simulation that create fire or earthquakes. These questions are better
  explored through fiction than philosophy alone.

I am pretty sure that I will dive into some of these tropes and I already got
started with some fragments that could easily bend into this direction. Let's
see what my personal simulation, the one in my head has to offer up next.

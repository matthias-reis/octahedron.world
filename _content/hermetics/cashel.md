Sightseeing is weird and funny for insiders like us. They don't know anything,
these poor normal souls who stand arund with me in these old ruins. They admire
the fading relics for a meaning they don't understand. Or for their morbid
aestehetics. Or because someone told them anbout how important this was. Even
after 12.000 years they don't have the slightest clue that we exist. We, the
Hermetics.

A few yeras ago for example, I visited the Rock of Cashel in Ireland. When you
look at the stone carvings and the other details in that area, you find hints
everywhere. At least three generations of the great and famous order of St.
Patrick had left their traces there.

Had I been alone, I could have easily opened three secret passages to hidden,
underground miracles. Well, I wasn't. There were several dozens of civilians and
I dind't want to break the second rule. However, I found some interesting
inscriptions from the 12th century with a clear hermetic target audience. If
anyone else noiced, they would see the skull relief as just another curiosity.
But I could tell a different story.

The early Gothic times from the 12th and 13th century were the Golden Age of the
Hermetic Order of St. Patrick. The order still exists today with fifteen
branches and is spread around Ireland and Northern Ireland. The Order watched
over the Irish Island during hard times with the plague and starvation period.
BUt the Hermetics, closely entangled with the catholic church over here didn't
show much mercy, barely offered help if their organisation wouldn't profit from
it.

The Rock was usually inhabited by two of the eight grand masters of the order.
They also had civil titles such as baron, arch bishop or marquesse. But their
actual power spread from the order. This situation was typical for the medieval
period all over europe, a static power system steered by the church and kept
alive by some of the hermetic orders.

Also the region around the Rock is what the Academy calls a Mana Center, a place
where several ley lines cross. The rock itself became one of the four biggest
hermetic centers in Europe and after Stonehenge the biggest on the british
isles. Some of the hermetic strongholds are still very active today. There is a
magic wall around the Rock of Cashel in about 2 kilometers distance that was
created to keep the enemies out, which still spreads its mana today. And the
abbey close by, theo only other building inside that ring of mana, spreads even
stronger vibes showing the sheer power that was cooking there.

Now I'm looking at a group of almost unrecognizable runes on a stone in the
middle of the runis of that old cathedral right between gravestones of old
grandmasters. These inscriptions only known by insiders like us still show the
way to the intact catacombs of the Order of St. Patrick - still intact and still
in use by the order today.
